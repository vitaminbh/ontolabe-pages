Privacy Policy for fieldscope

Last updated: July 7, 2026

fieldscope is a field-instrument panel app. This policy explains what data it uses and, just as importantly, what it does not.

Location

fieldscope requests access to your device’s location solely to compute local sunrise, sunset, and moon phase for your current position. This calculation happens entirely on your device. Your location is never transmitted to fieldscope’s developer or any third party, and it is never stored or retained.

Other on-device sensors

fieldscope may also read on-device sensors you enable — the barometer, magnetometer, and microphone (for ambient sound level) — to display live readings in the app. These readings stay on your device and are used only to render the instrument panel and, optionally, a local history you control.

Network requests

Some instruments in fieldscope fetch public data feeds (for example, space-weather indices, seismic activity, and news/RSS feeds) so they can be displayed alongside your local sensor readings. These requests fetch public data only — they do not send your location, sensor readings, or any other personal data.

Data collection

fieldscope does not collect, transmit, sell, or share personal data. It does not use analytics or advertising SDKs, and it does not track you across apps or websites.
